The seventh united nations goal is to develop affordable and clean energy worldwide. This is one of the most important goals because climate change is the biggest threat to humans in our history. If we take advantage of our earth we won't be able to keep it for long. 89% of our energy comes from non renewable sources, and that needs to change.

Did you know that Iceland is one of the most eco-friendly countries on earth in energy usage? 99% of Icelands heating and cooling systems come from geothermal energy, a non fossil fuel source. Iceland has been a pioneer of energy usage and humans globally should be trying to match them

Since the late 19th century, coal has been used across the globe to power trains, homes and plants for a cheap price effectively. But not until now have fossil fuels been discovered to be killing the only planet we have. Fossil fuels generate a substancial amount of carbon dioxide gas, which creates a greenhouse effect, thus ruining habitats, food sources, and lives

Right now, fossil fuels make up around 82% of the world's total energy sources. This is absolutely detremential to the health and well being of our earth, animals and other living things, and even ourselves. Luckily, the use is expected to decline to about 78% by 2040, putting it at a decline.

The main problem with coal and fossil fuel use is the greenhouse effect. This comes from fossil fuels generating too much carbon dioxide gas, which stays in our inner atmosphere, trapping heat from escaping the earth. This causes the earth to heat up at an unnatural rate. This extra heat melts polar ice caps, ruins homes and kills off food sources.
Renewable energy sources. These sources include wind, solar, water, and geothermal energy sources. If we were to invest and focus on these sources, it would decrease the amount of carbon dioxide in our atmosphere, lowering our global rising temperature, and thus saving our earth from a future of horror.
